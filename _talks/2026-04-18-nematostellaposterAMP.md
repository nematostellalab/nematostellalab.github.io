abstract: "Animals have unique and different physiological and behavioral responses to environmental stimuli. In _Nematostella_ sea anemones, salinity levels are one of the external stimuli that trigger a response to different salinity levels, primarily through osmotic stress, activation of molecular pathways, and calcium signaling. Here, we utilize the sea anemone _Nematostella_ to compare behavioral and molecular responses of individuals exposed to different levels of salinity by performing a “death hang” experiment, which consists of putting anemones in just enough water for them not to die, while trying to get them to the stress mode to see if any responses appear, expecting to see some behavioral changes, such as shrinking their bodies and tentacles inside, and the production of mucus."
